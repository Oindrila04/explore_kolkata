Welcome to the city masked with festivals throughout the year!


Kolkata is the cultural capital of India. There’s a saying in Bengali that goes, baro mashe tero parbon, literally ‘13 festivals in 12 months’, neatly summing up the importance — and yearlong abundance — of festivals in Kolkata’s everyday life. From cultural extravaganzas to some of India's biggest Hindu festivals, there's always something going on in the city.


The entire atmosphere is painted completely different during the festivals and there is a lot of commotion and rush all around.


Lets dive into the festivities!

Durga Puja

Durga Puja, held in September–October, is the biggest and most important festival of West Bengal as well as of Kolkata. It marks the victory of ten-armed goddess Durga over the buffalo demon Mahishasur and signifies the victory of good over evil.Some 3000 pandals are erected throughout Kolkata and its suburbs. Pujas are also conducted in sizeable number of family households.


Although the root of Durga Puja is essentially religious, the festival has slowly changed into more of a social carnival than a religious festival, where people from other religious or ethnic backgrounds freely participate in the festivity. It has become Kolkata's biggest public spectacle, glamorous art event and consumerist carnival. The city is decked up with lighting decorations. Loudspeakers play popular songs as well as recitation of mantras by priests. Shops, restaurants and eateries stay open all night. Fairs are set up in numerous parks and public spaces. The roads become overcrowded with hundreds of thousands of devotees, revellers and pandal-hoppers visiting the pandals on Puja days.


On Bijaya Dashami, the day of the festival, people bid adieu to goddess Durga offering her sweets and sindoor. Before bidding adieu to the goddess, the married women assemble near the idol, apply vermilion on her feet or forehead and then smear vermillion on each other. This is a part of the last celebration before the idols are taken out in grand processions of song, dance, music and then immersed in the waters. The idol immersion ceremony continues for the next few days. People distribute sweets and visit their friends, family members and relatives to exchange the Bijoya greetings.

The Red Road Carnival

Durga Puja is an emotion that never ends. And keeping this emotion intact, the state government every year gives a befitting end to the Durga Puja celebrations through a fabulous Durga Puja Carnival. According to the state information and culture department, around 80 idols from Kolkata and various district puja committees become part of this carnival.


Durga idols of 68 leading community pujas of the city and the districts, accompanied by tableaux presenting the unique and intricate artwork at the marquees and dazzling illumination take part in the 'Red Road Carnival', marking the end of Durga Puja festivities.

Kolkata International Book Fair

The 'Kolkata International Book Fair or Kolkata Boi Mela is the world's largest non-trade annual book fair as well as the largest book fair in Asia. Held on the Milan Mela ground, this fair has over 600 stalls, selling over Rs.23,000,000 worth of books and attracting more than 2.5 million visitors annually.


It offers a typical fairground experience with a literary theme with picknickers, singer-songwriters, artistes and candyfloss vendors. It starts on the last Wednesday of January, and continues for twelve days, including two weekends.

Christmas in Kolkata

Christmas is celebrated with so much pomp and joy, that the city becomes one of the most merry cities in during this time. The shimmering lights, Christmas songs, rock music, scrumptious food, the trees, the high spirits of people, gifts, presents, aroma of fruit cake and pastries, make the City of Joy livelier and brighter during the Christmas season. Kolkata’s Christmas celebrations makes the festive mood extravagant, as not only Christians, but people from different communities, celebrate the festival with joy, and offer prayers and sing carols during the midnight mass in the churches.


Christmas and Park Street, both are synonym to each other in Kolkata. Live performances at Allen Park, food extravaganza, courtesy food kiosks dishing up an array of items, crowds of people, loud music and songs, different stalls selling Christmas goodies, the beautiful twinkling lights, makes Park Street the most lit up place in Kolkata


The largest and the most popular cathedral in Kolkata, holds the midnight mass, with music by children’s choirs and carols. The church is decorated beautifully to celebrate the occasion. Adorned with Christmas tree, lights, silver bells and streamers, it hosts a grand celebration. People from different faiths and backgrounds come together to celebrate the festival.

Kolkata International Film Festival

The Kolkata International Film Festival (KIFF) is an annual film festival held in Kolkata. Founded in 1995, it is the third oldest international film festival in India. The festival is organized by the West Bengal Film Centre under the West Bengal Government.


Durations are not less than 60 minutes for featured films. The length of short films are not more than 30 minutes, and for documentaries not more than 60 minutes. Productions are not older than two years and are an Indian premiere
